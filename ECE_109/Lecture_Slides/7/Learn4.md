# Learn4: Atomic Mass, Mole, and Bonding Types
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: Atomic Mass and Mole Concepts

**Atomic mass unit (u)**: $u = 1.66054 \times 10^{-27}$ kg
- Defined as 1/12 of the mass of a neutral carbon-12 atom

**Avogadro's number**: $N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23}$
- Number of atoms in exactly 12 grams of carbon-12

**Atomic mass (atomic weight)**: Average atomic mass in atomic mass units of all naturally occurring isotopes

**Mole**: Amount of substance containing exactly $N_A$ atoms or molecules

## Section 2: Weight and Atomic Fraction Conversion

When an alloy or compound has two elements A and B:

**Weight fractions**: $w_A + w_B = 1$
**Atomic fractions**: $n_A + n_B = 1$

Conversion from weight fraction to atomic fraction:
$$n_A = \frac{w_A/M_A}{w_A/M_A + w_B/M_B}$$
$$n_B = 1 - n_A$$

Where $M_A$ and $M_B$ are the atomic masses.

## Section 3: Interatomic Bonding

When atoms approach each other, attractive forces (FA) and repulsive forces (FR) act between them. At equilibrium separation $r_o$, the net force is zero.

The bond energy $E_{bond}$ is the energy released when atoms form a bond.

## Section 4: Types of Bonding

**Ionic Bonding**:
- Transfer of electrons from metal to non-metal
- Example: NaCl - Na gives electron to Cl
- Creates positive ions (cations) and negative ions (anions)
- Bonding due to Coulombic attraction between ions
- High melting point, brittle, poor electrical conductivity

**Covalent Bonding**:
- Sharing of valence electrons between atoms
- Example: H₂, CH₄, Diamond (C)
- Each atom achieves closed electron shells through sharing
- Directional bonds (109.5° in methane)
- Strong bonds → high melting point, hard, brittle
- Poor electrical conductivity (electrons locked in bonds)

**Metallic Bonding**:
- Metal atoms lose valence electrons
- Free electrons form electron gas/cloud
- Positive metal ion cores held together by attraction to electron gas
- Non-directional bonding
- Good electrical and thermal conductivity
- Malleable and ductile

## Key Equations

Atomic mass unit:
$$u = 1.66054 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$$

Avogadro's number:
$$N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1}$$

Weight to atomic fraction:
$$n_A = \frac{w_A/M_A}{w_A/M_A + w_B/M_B}$$

Equilibrium condition:
$$F_N = F_A + F_R = 0$$

Where:
- $w_A, w_B$ = weight fractions
- $n_A, n_B$ = atomic (molar) fractions
- $M_A, M_B$ = atomic masses (g/mol)
