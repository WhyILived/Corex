## Conceptual Questions

1. Why does covalent bonding only occur between atoms with similar electronegativity?

2. Why do metals conduct electricity while covalent solids generally do not?

3. In metallic bonding, why are metals malleable (can be hammered into shapes) while ionic and covalent solids are brittle?

4. Why is the bond angle in methane (CH₄) 109.5° rather than 90°?

## Calculation Problems

1. **Pb-Sn Solder Conversion**
   A Pb-Sn solder contains 38.1 wt% Pb and 61.9 wt% Sn.
   
   Calculate the atomic fraction of Pb and Sn.
   
   Given: $M_{Pb} = 207.2$ g/mol, $M_{Sn} = 118.71$ g/mol

2. **Mole Calculation**
   How many atoms are in 5 grams of aluminum (Al, atomic mass = 26.98 g/mol)?

3. **Atomic Mass Calculation**
   Naturally occurring chlorine is 75.77% Cl-35 (mass = 34.97 u) and 24.23% Cl-37 (mass = 36.97 u).
   
   Calculate the average atomic mass of chlorine.

## Answers

1. For Pb-Sn solder:
   
   $n_{Pb} = \frac{0.381/207.2}{0.381/207.2 + 0.619/118.71} = \frac{0.001839}{0.001839 + 0.005214} = \frac{0.001839}{0.007053} \approx 0.261$ (26.1 at.%)
   
   $n_{Sn} = 1 - 0.261 = 0.739$ (73.9 at.%)

2. $n = 5 \text{ g} / 26.98 \text{ g/mol} = 0.185$ mol
   
   Atoms = $0.185 \times 6.022 \times 10^{23} \approx 1.11 \times 10^{23}$ atoms

3. Average atomic mass = $0.7577 \times 34.97 + 0.2423 \times 36.97$
   
   $= 26.50 + 8.96 \approx 35.46$ u
