# Quick Review - Lecture 7

## Finite Potential Well
- Wavefunction penetrates barriers exponentially (decay constant α)
- Penetration depth $x_o = 1/\alpha$
- Energy levels lower than infinite well of same width
- Boundary conditions: ψ and dψ/dx continuous

## Quantum Tunneling
- Classically forbidden but quantum mechanically possible
- Transmission probability $T \propto \exp(-2\alpha a)$
- Wider/higher barriers → exponentially smaller tunneling probability
- Applications: STM, semiconductor devices

## Infinite Potential Box - Three Quantum Numbers
- Three independent quantum numbers n₁, n₂, n₃ (1,2,3...)
- Wavefunction is product of sin functions
- Energy depends on sum of squared quantum numbers
- Degeneracy: different sets can give same energy

## Hydrogen Atom
- Three quantum numbers: n (energy), ℓ (shape), mℓ (orientation)
- n = 1,2,3...; ℓ = 0,1,2...,n-1; mℓ = -ℓ to +ℓ
- Energy $E_n = -13.6/n^2$ eV
- 1s orbital max probability at Bohr radius $a_o = 0.0529$ nm

## Virial Theorem
- $KE = -PE/2$ (for bound systems)
- Total energy E = KE + PE = PE/2 = -KE

## Atomic Structure
- Z = protons, A = protons + neutrons
- Ionization energy: remove electron
- Electron affinity: add electron
- Max electrons in shell n: 2n²

## Atomic Mass and Mole
- 1 u = $1.66 \times 10^{-27}$ kg (1/12 carbon-12)
- 1 mole = $N_A$ particles = mass in grams = atomic weight
- Weight ↔ Atomic fraction: $n_A = (w_A/M_A)/(w_A/M_A + w_B/M_B)$

## Bonding Types
- **Ionic**: Electron transfer → ions → Coulomb attraction
- **Covalent**: Electron sharing → directional bonds
- **Metallic**: Delocalized electron gas → non-directional
