# Test2: Infinite Potential Box - Three Quantum Numbers
> Tests: Learn2 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. Why does a particle in a 3D box have three quantum numbers instead of one?

2. Why can quantum numbers n₁, n₂, n₃ never be zero?

3. Different sets of quantum numbers can give the same energy value. Explain with an example from a cubic box.

## Calculation Problems

1. **Energy Levels in a Cubic Box**
   An electron is confined in a cubic box with side length a = 0.5 nm.
   
   a) Calculate the ground state energy E₁₁₁.
   
   b) List all distinct sets of quantum numbers that give N² = n₁² + n₂² + n₃² = 14.
   
   c) What is the energy of the first excited state (lowest energy above ground state)?

   Given: $h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s, $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg

2. **Rectangular Box**
   A particle is in a rectangular box with a = 0.3 nm, b = 0.4 nm, c = 0.5 nm.
   
   Calculate the ratio of energies E₂₂₂ : E₁₁₁.

3. **Degeneracy**
   For a cubic box, how many distinct quantum number sets give N² = 41? List them.

## Answers

1. a) $E_{111} = \frac{h^2(1+1+1)}{8m_ea^2} = \frac{3h^2}{8m_ea^2} = \frac{3(6.63 \times 10^{-34})^2}{8(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(0.5 \times 10^{-9})^2} \approx 7.3 \times 10^{-19}$ J ≈ 4.6 eV

   b) N² = 14: (3,2,1), (3,1,2), (2,3,1), (2,1,3), (1,3,2), (1,2,3) - all permutations of 3,2,1

   c) First excited state: N² = 6 → (2,1,1) and permutations
   $E_{211} = \frac{6h^2}{8m_ea^2} = 2 \times E_{111}$

2. $E_{222}/E_{111} = (4/a^2 + 4/b^2 + 4/c^2) / (1/a^2 + 1/b^2 + 1/c^2) = 4$

3. N² = 41: From lecture - (4,4,3), (4,3,4), (3,4,4) give 3 states; (6,2,1) and permutations give 6 states. Total 9 distinct states.
