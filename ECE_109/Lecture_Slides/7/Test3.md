# Test3: Hydrogen Atom and Atomic Structure
> Tests: Learn3 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. Why does the hydrogen atom have an infinite number of allowed energy states?

2. Why are there no 1p or 2d orbitals?

3. Sketch the form of the radial probability density P(r) for the 1s, 2s, and 2p states. Why does P(r) go to zero at r = 0 for all states?

4. According to the virial theorem, if the total energy of an electron is -5 eV, what are its kinetic and potential energies?

## Calculation Problems

1. **Hydrogen Energy Levels**
   Calculate the wavelength of light emitted when an electron transitions from n=3 to n=2 in hydrogen.
   
   Hint: $E_n = -13.6 \text{ eV}/n^2$ and $E = hc/\lambda$
   
   Given: $h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s, $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s, 1 eV = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ J

2. **Quantum Numbers**
   For n = 3:
   a) List all allowed values of ℓ
   b) For each ℓ, list all allowed values of $m_\ell$
   c) How many total orbitals are there at n = 3?

3. **Virial Theorem Application**
   The ionization energy of Li²⁺ (Z=3, helium-like ion) is 122.4 eV.
   a) What is the energy of the ground state (n=1)?
   b) What is the radius of the ground state orbit?
   c) What are the average PE and KE of the electron?
   
   Hint: For hydrogenic atoms, $E_n = -13.6 Z^2/n^2$ eV and $r_n = a_o/n^2$

## Answers

1. $\Delta E = |E_2 - E_3| = |-13.6/4 - (-13.6/9)| = |-3.4 + 1.51| = 1.89$ eV (emitted photon energy)
   
   $\lambda = hc/\Delta E = (6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^8) / (1.89 \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \approx 656$ nm (red light)

2. For n = 3:
   a) ℓ = 0, 1, 2
   b) ℓ=0: $m_\ell$ = 0; ℓ=1: $m_\ell$ = -1, 0, +1; ℓ=2: $m_\ell$ = -2, -1, 0, +1, +2
   c) Total orbitals = 1 + 3 + 5 = 9 orbitals (18 electrons maximum)

3. Li²⁺ with Z=3:
   a) $E_1 = -13.6 \times 9 = -122.4$ eV
   b) $r_1 = a_o/3 = 0.0529/3 = 0.0176$ nm (higher Z pulls electrons into smaller radius)
   c) $PE = 2E = -244.8$ eV, $KE = 122.4$ eV
