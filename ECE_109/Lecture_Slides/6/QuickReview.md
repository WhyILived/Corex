# Quick Review - Lecture 6

## Key Concepts

**Stationary States:** Eigenstates of the Hamiltonian that evolve in time by a phase factor. The probability density $|\psi|^2$ is time-independent.

**Quantum Confinement:** When electrons are confined to dimensions comparable to the de Broglie wavelength, their energy becomes quantized.

**Infinite vs Finite Well:**
- Infinite well: Wavefunction is exactly zero at boundaries, analytical solutions
- Finite well: Wavefunction decays exponentially outside, graphical solution required

**Tunneling:** Quantum phenomenon where particles can pass through barriers even when $E < V_0$. Probability decreases exponentially with barrier width.

**STM:** Scanning Tunneling Microscope uses electron tunneling between a probe and sample to achieve atomic-resolution imaging.

**Degeneracy:** In 3D, multiple quantum number combinations can yield the same energy (e.g., states $(2,1,1)$, $(1,2,1)$, $(1,1,2)$ in a cubic box).

| Tunneling probability | $T \approx e^{-2\alpha a}$ |
| 3D box energy | $E \propto (n_1^2 + n_2^2 + n_3^2)$ |
| H-atom quantum numbers | $n, \ell, m_\ell$ |

## Remember

- Stationary states have constant $|\psi|^2$ in time
- Penetration depth $x_0 = 1/\alpha$ describes tunneling
- Three quantum numbers needed in 3D confinement
- H-atom: $n$ controls energy, $\ell$ controls shape, $m_\ell$ controls orientation
