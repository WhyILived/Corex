# Test2: Energy Quantization and the Photoelectric Effect
> Tests: Learn2 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. What is the physical meaning of Planck's constant $h$?

2. Why does increasing light intensity NOT increase the kinetic energy of photoelectrons?

3. What is the relationship between the work function and threshold frequency?

## Calculation Problems

1. Calculate the energy of a photon with frequency $6 \times 10^{14}$ Hz. Express your answer in both Joules and electron-volts.

2. The work function of sodium is approximately 2.38 eV.
   - What is the threshold wavelength for photoemission from sodium?
   - Will green light (λ = 522 nm) cause photoemission from sodium?

3. UV light with wavelength 250 nm strikes a clean sodium surface.
   - Calculate the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons in eV.
   - What stopping voltage would be needed to prevent these electrons from reaching the anode?

Given: $\Phi_{sodium} = 2.38$ eV

## Answers

1. $E = hf = (6.6 \times 10^{-34})(6 \times 10^{14}) = 3.96 \times 10^{-19}$ J
   
   Converting to eV: $E = (3.96 \times 10^{-19})/(1.6 \times 10^{-19}) = 2.48$ eV

2.
   - $\lambda_0 = hc/\Phi = (1240 \text{ eV·nm})/(2.38 \text{ eV}) = 521$ nm
   - Green light (522 nm) is just slightly above the threshold, so yes, it will cause photoemission.

3.
   - $E_{photon} = hc/\lambda = (1240)/(250) = 4.96$ eV
   - $K_{E,max} = 4.96 - 2.38 = 2.58$ eV
   - $V_0 = K_{E,max}/e = 2.58$ V (to stop electrons with 2.58 eV kinetic energy)
