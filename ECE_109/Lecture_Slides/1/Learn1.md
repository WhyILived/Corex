# Learn1: Wave Equation and Light as Electromagnetic Waves
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: What is a Wave?
A wave is a periodically repeating disturbance in space and time that propagates through a medium. For light, the wave consists of oscillating electric and magnetic field strengths.

## Section 2: The Wave Equation
The fundamental wave equation describing light propagation is:

$$\nabla^2 u - \frac{1}{c^2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial t^2}u = 0$$

For the electric field in one dimension:

$$\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}E(z,t) - \frac{1}{c^2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial t^2}E(z,t) = 0$$

Where:
- $\nabla^2$ is the Laplacian operator
- $c$ is the speed of light in vacuum ($3 \times 10^8$ m/s)
- $u$ represents the wave displacement (can be a scalar or vector)

## Section 3: Light as an Electromagnetic Wave
Light is an electromagnetic wave where the electric and magnetic fields oscillate perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation.

The electric field at position $z$ and time $t$ for a monochromatic plane wave is:

$$E(z,t) = E_0\cos(kz - \omega t + \phi_0)$$

Where:
- $E_0$ = amplitude of the electric field
- $k$ = wavenumber = $2\pi/\lambda$
- $\omega$ = angular frequency = $2\pi f$
- $\phi_0$ = phase constant
- $\lambda$ = wavelength
- $f$ = frequency

## Section 4: Key Wave Properties
- **Wavelength ($\lambda$)**: Spatial period - distance over which the wave repeats
- **Period ($T$)**: Temporal period - time for one complete oscillation
- **Frequency ($f$)**: Number of oscillations per unit time; $f = 1/T$
- **Wavenumber ($k$)**: $k = 2\pi/\lambda$ (spatial angular frequency)
- **Angular frequency ($\omega$)**: $\omega = 2\pi f$

## Section 5: Phase Velocity
The speed at which a point of constant phase travels:

$$v = \frac{\omega}{k} = f\lambda$$

For light in vacuum, $v = c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s.

## Key Equations
$$\frac{\partial^2}{\partial z^2}E(z,t) - \frac{1}{c^2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial t^2}E(z,t) = 0$$

$$\omega = 2\pi f$$

$$k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$$

$$v = \frac{\omega}{k} = f\lambda$$

## Supporting Points from Lecture
- Light exhibits wave properties: interference and diffraction
- Light rays are perpendicular to wave fronts
- A beam is a bundle of rays
- Maxwell's equations describe all electromagnetic phenomena
