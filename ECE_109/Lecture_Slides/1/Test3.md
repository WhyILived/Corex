# Test3: Photon Properties - Energy, Momentum, and Light Intensity
> Tests: Learn3 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. How does the photon view of light explain why increasing light intensity increases photocurrent but not electron energy?

2. What is the relationship between photon energy and wavelength? Between photon momentum and wavelength?

3. Distinguish between light intensity and photon flux density.

## Calculation Problems

1. Calculate the momentum of a photon with wavelength 600 nm.

2. Blue light (λ = 450 nm) has an intensity of 10 W/m². Calculate:
   - The energy of one blue photon
   - The photon flux density (photons per m² per second)

3. Compare the momentum of a photon with λ = 500 nm to the momentum of an electron moving at $10^6$ m/s.

Given: $m_e = 9.1 \times 10^{-31}$ kg, $h = 6.6 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s

## Answers

1. $p = h/\lambda = (6.6 \times 10^{-34})/(600 \times 10^{-9}) = 1.1 \times 10^{-27}$ kg·m/s

2.
   - $E_{ph} = hc/\lambda = (6.6 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^8)/(450 \times 10^{-9}) = 4.4 \times 10^{-19}$ J
   - $\Gamma_{ph} = I/E_{ph} = 10/(4.4 \times 10^{-19}) = 2.3 \times 10^{19}$ photons/m²/s

3.
   - Photon: $p_{ph} = h/\lambda = (6.6 \times 10^{-34})/(500 \times 10^{-9}) = 1.32 \times 10^{-27}$ kg·m/s
   - Electron: $p_e = m_ev = (9.1 \times 10^{-31})(10^6) = 9.1 \times 10^{-25}$ kg·m/s
   - The electron momentum is much larger (~700×) than the photon momentum at this wavelength.
