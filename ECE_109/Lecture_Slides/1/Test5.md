# Test5: Stimulated Emission and Lasers
> Tests: Learn5 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. Explain the difference between spontaneous emission and stimulated emission. Why is stimulated emission essential for laser operation?

2. Why is population inversion necessary for laser action? Why can't we achieve it with just two energy levels?

3. What is a metastable state? Why is it important for lasers?

4. Describe the three main components of any laser system and their functions.

5. What makes laser light different from light from a regular light bulb?

## Calculation Problems

1. **Ruby Laser Wavelength**
   - The ruby laser emits at 694.3 nm.
   - Calculate the energy difference between the excited and ground state in electron-volts.
   - Given: $hc = 1240$ eV·nm

2. **HeNe Laser Efficiency**
   - A HeNe laser operates at 2 kV and 5 mA, producing 2.5 mW of output power.
   - Calculate its efficiency.

3. **Photon Flux**
   - A 1 mW laser pointer emits at 650 nm.
   - How many photons per second does it emit?

4. **Wavelength Comparison**
   - Calculate the wavelength for transitions corresponding to:
     (a) $E = 2$ eV (visible red)
     (b) $E = 3$ eV (visible blue)
   - Which color has higher energy photons?

## Answers

1. **Ruby Laser Energy:**
   - $E = hc/\lambda = 1240/694.3 = 1.79$ eV

2. **HeNe Efficiency:**
   - Input power = $(2000 \text{ V})(0.005 \text{ A}) = 10$ W
   - Efficiency = Output/Input = $0.0025/10 = 0.00025 = 0.025\%$

3. **Photon Flux:**
   - Energy per photon at 650 nm: $E = hc/\lambda = 1240/650 = 1.91$ eV $= 3.06 \times 10^{-19}$ J
   - Photons/second = $P/E = (0.001)/(3.06 \times 10^{-19}) = 3.27 \times 10^{15}$ photons/s

4. **Wavelength Calculation:**
   - (a) $\lambda = hc/E = 1240/2 = 620$ nm (red-orange)
   - (b) $\lambda = 1240/3 = 413$ nm (violet-blue)
   - Blue photons (3 eV) have higher energy than red photons (2 eV)
