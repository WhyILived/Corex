# Test3: Light Quantities - Energy, Momentum, and Intensity
> Tests: Learn3 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions
1. Write the equation for photon energy in terms of frequency and in terms of wavelength. Which form is more useful for high-energy photons and why?
2. Light of a given wavelength has a specific momentum. Explain why a massless particle can have momentum.
3. Distinguish between light intensity and photon flux density.

## Calculation Problems
1. Calculate the momentum of a photon with wavelength 500 nm.
   - Answer: $p = \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-34}}{500 \times 10^{-9}} = 1.33 \times 10^{-27}$ kg·m/s

2. Light with wavelength 550 nm and intensity 100 W/m² shines on a surface.
   - (a) Calculate the photon energy.
   - (b) Calculate the photon flux density.
   - Answers: (a) $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{(6.63 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{550 \times 10^{-9}} = 3.62 \times 10^{-19}$ J; (b) $\Gamma_{ph} = \frac{I}{E} = \frac{100}{3.62 \times 10^{-19}} = 2.76 \times 10^{20}$ photons/m²/s

3. Compare the momentum of a photon (λ = 400 nm) to the momentum of an electron moving at $1 \times 10^6$ m/s.
   - Photon: $p_\gamma = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-34}}{400 \times 10^{-9}} = 1.66 \times 10^{-27}$ kg·m/s
   - Electron: $p_e = m_e v = (9.11 \times 10^{-31})(10^6) = 9.11 \times 10^{-25}$ kg·m/s
   - Answer: The electron's momentum is about 550 times larger than the photon's momentum.

## Answers
1. $E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$. For high-energy photons (short wavelength like X-rays), the wavelength form is more convenient because $hc \approx 1240$ eV·nm gives direct energy in eV when $\lambda$ is in nm.
2. Photon momentum $p = h/\lambda$ arises from the wave nature of light. Although photons have zero rest mass, they carry energy and momentum. This was confirmed by Compton scattering experiments where photons transfer momentum to electrons.
3. Light intensity $I$ is the energy per unit area per unit time (W/m²). Photon flux density $\Gamma_{ph}$ is the number of photons per unit area per unit time. They are related by $I = \Gamma_{ph} \cdot hf$.
