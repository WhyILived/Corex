# Learn3: Light Quantities - Energy, Momentum, and Intensity
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: Photon Energy and Momentum
- Photon energy: $E_{photon} = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \hbar\omega$
- Photon momentum: $p = \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{hf}{c} = \frac{E_{photon}}{c}$
- These relate wave properties ($\lambda$, $f$) to particle properties ($E$, $p$)

## Section 2: Light Intensity
- Light intensity $I$ is the energy flowing per unit area per unit time
- Classical wave interpretation: $I = \frac{1}{2}c\epsilon_0 E_0^2$
- Photon (particle) interpretation: $I = \Gamma_{ph} hf$
- Photon flux density: $\Gamma_{ph} = \frac{\Delta N_{ph}}{A \Delta t}$ (number of photons crossing unit area per unit time)

## Section 3: Wave-Particle Duality
- Light exhibits both wave-like properties (interference, diffraction) and particle-like properties (photoelectric effect, Compton scattering)
- The photon is a massless particle carrying quantized energy $hf$ and momentum $h/\lambda$

## Key Equations
$$E_{photon} = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \hbar\omega$$

Where:
- $E_{photon}$ = photon energy (J)
- $h$ = Planck's constant ($6.63 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s)
- $f$ = frequency (Hz)
- $\hbar = h/2\pi = 1.05 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s (reduced Planck's constant)
- $\omega = 2\pi f$ = angular frequency (rad/s)

$$p_{photon} = \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{hf}{c} = \frac{E_{photon}}{c}$$

Where:
- $p_{photon}$ = photon momentum (kg·m/s)

$$I = \frac{1}{2}c\epsilon_0 E_0^2$$

Where:
- $I$ = light intensity (W/m²)
- $c$ = speed of light ($3 \times 10^8$ m/s)
- $\epsilon_0$ = vacuum permittivity ($8.85 \times 10^{-12}$ F/m)
- $E_0$ = peak electric field amplitude (V/m)

$$I = \Gamma_{ph} hf = \Gamma_{ph} E_{photon}$$

Where:
- $\Gamma_{ph}$ = photon flux density (photons/m²/s)
- $hf$ = energy per photon (J)

$$\Gamma_{ph} = \frac{\Delta N_{ph}}{A \Delta t}$$

Where:
- $\Delta N_{ph}$ = number of photons crossing area $A$ in time $\Delta t$
