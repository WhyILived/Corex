# Test2: Photoelectric Effect and Photon Concept
> Tests: Learn2 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions
1. State Einstein's photoelectric equation and explain what each term represents.
2. Why does increasing light intensity increase photocurrent but not the kinetic energy of emitted electrons?
3. What determines whether photoemission occurs for a given metal?
4. What is the physical meaning of the work function?

## Calculation Problems
1. Calculate the energy of a photon with wavelength 600 nm in both joules and electron-volts.
   - Answer: $E = \frac{(6.63 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{600 \times 10^{-9}} = 3.32 \times 10^{-19}$ J $= 2.07$ eV

2. Sodium has a work function of 2.28 eV.
   - (a) What is the threshold wavelength for photoemission?
   - (b) If light of 400 nm shines on sodium, what is the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons?
   - Answers: (a) $\lambda_0 = \frac{hc}{\Phi} = \frac{1240 \text{ eV·nm}}{2.28 \text{ eV}} = 544$ nm; (b) $KE = \frac{1240}{400} - 2.28 = 3.10 - 2.28 = 0.82$ eV

3. Light with wavelength 450 nm and intensity 10 W/m² strikes a metal with work function 2.0 eV.
   - Calculate the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons.
   - Answer: $E_{photon} = \frac{1240}{450} = 2.76$ eV, so $KE_{max} = 2.76 - 2.0 = 0.76$ eV

## Answers
1. $KE_{max} = hf - \Phi = hf - hf_0$, where $KE_{max}$ is the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons, $h$ is Planck's constant, $f$ is the light frequency, $\Phi$ is the work function, and $f_0$ is the threshold frequency.
2. Light intensity is proportional to photon flux (number of photons per area per time). More intensity means more photons, hence more electrons emitted. But each photon's energy depends only on frequency ($hf$), so electron energy is unaffected by intensity.
3. The light frequency must be greater than the threshold frequency $f_0$ (or equivalently, photon energy must exceed the work function $\Phi$).
4. The work function $\Phi$ is the minimum energy required to remove an electron from the metal surface.
