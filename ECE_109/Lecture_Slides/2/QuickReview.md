# Quick Review - Lecture 2

## Wave-Particle Duality
Light exhibits both wave and particle properties. Matter (electrons, etc.) also exhibits wave properties.

## Key Concepts

**Light as a Wave:**
- Electromagnetic waves described by $E(x,t) = E_0 \cos(kx - \omega t)$
- Interference from double-slit: constructive ($S_1P - S_2P = n\lambda$), destructive ($S_1P - S_2P = (n+1/2)\lambda$)
- Diffraction follows Bragg's law: $2d\sin\theta = n\lambda$

**Light as a Particle (Photons):**
- Photon energy: $E = hf = hc/\lambda$
- Photon momentum: $p = h/\lambda$
- Photoelectric effect: $KE_{max} = hf - \Phi$; depends on frequency, not intensity

**Electron as a Wave:**
- De Broglie wavelength: $\lambda = h/p$
- Confirmed by electron diffraction experiments (Young's slits, crystal diffraction)
- For voltage-accelerated electrons: $\lambda = h/\sqrt{2m_e eV}$

**Light Intensity:**
- Wave interpretation: $I = \frac{1}{2}c\epsilon_0 E_0^2$
- Particle interpretation: $I = \Gamma_{ph} hf$ (photon flux × energy per photon)

## What to Remember
- Wave-particle duality is fundamental to quantum physics
- De Broglie relations connect particle momentum to wave wavelength
- Bragg's law explains diffraction patterns from crystals
- Photoelectric effect demonstrates particle nature of light
