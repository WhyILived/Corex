# Test1: Wave Interference and Young's Double-Slit Experiment
> Tests: Learn1 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions
1. What is the relationship between wavelength, frequency, and the speed of light for an electromagnetic wave?
2. State the condition for constructive interference in Young's double-slite experiment in terms of path difference.
3. State the condition for destructive interference in Young's double-slite experiment in terms of path difference.
4. Why does interference demonstrate that light is a wave?

## Calculation Problems
1. Light with wavelength 550 nm passes through a double-slit with slit separation 0.10 mm.
   - Calculate the angle to the third-order bright fringe.
   - Answer: $\sin\theta = \frac{3\lambda}{d} = \frac{3(550 \times 10^{-9})}{0.10 \times 10^{-3}} = 0.0165$, so $\theta \approx 0.95°$

2. In a double-slit experiment, the distance between slits is 0.20 mm and the screen is 1.5 m away. If the second bright fringe is 8.0 mm from the central maximum, calculate the wavelength of the light.
   - Answer: $\lambda = \frac{d \cdot y}{nL} = \frac{(0.20 \times 10^{-3})(8.0 \times 10^{-3})}{2(1.5)} = 5.33 \times 10^{-7}$ m = 533 nm

## Answers
1. $c = f\lambda$ - the speed of light equals frequency times wavelength
2. Path difference = $n\lambda$ where $n = 0, 1, 2, ...$
3. Path difference = $(n + \frac{1}{2})\lambda$ where $n = 0, 1, 2, ...$
4. Because interference patterns (bright and dark fringes) only occur when waves overlap and superpose. The formula $|\vec{E}_1 + \vec{E}_2|^2 \neq |\vec{E}_1|^2 + |\vec{E}_2|^2$ shows that energy is redistributed according to wave superposition, not added linearly.
