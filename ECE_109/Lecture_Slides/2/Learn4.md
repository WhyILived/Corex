# Learn4: De Broglie Relations - Electron as a Wave
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: Wave-Particle Duality for Matter
- If light (waves) can behave as particles (photons), then matter (particles) can behave as waves
- De Broglie hypothesis (1924): $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$ relates particle momentum to wave wavelength
- This applies to all particles: electrons, protons, atoms, and even macroscopic objects

## Section 2: Electron Diffraction and Interference
- Young's double-slit experiment with electrons produces interference fringes
- Electron beams passing through crystals produce diffraction patterns (Bragg scattering)
- Electron diffraction follows the Bragg condition: $2d\sin\theta = n\lambda$
- The diffraction pattern confirms electron wave behavior

## Section 3: Applications of Electron Waves
- Diffraction-based imaging: wavelength must be shorter than features to be resolved
- Electron diffraction used to study crystal structures (like X-ray diffraction)
- For electrons accelerated by voltage $V$: $KE = \frac{p^2}{2m_e} = eV$, so $\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2m_e eV}}$

## Key Equations
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} \quad \text{or} \quad p = \frac{h}{\lambda}$$

Where:
- $\lambda$ = de Broglie wavelength (m)
- $h$ = Planck's constant ($6.63 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s)
- $p$ = particle momentum (kg·m/s)

$$KE = \frac{p^2}{2m_e} = eV$$

Where:
- $KE$ = kinetic energy (J)
- $m_e$ = electron mass ($9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg)
- $e$ = electron charge ($1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C)
- $V$ = accelerating voltage (V)

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2m_e eV}}$$

For electron accelerated by voltage $V$:

$$2d\sin\theta = n\lambda \quad \text{(Bragg's law)}$$

Where:
- $d$ = spacing between crystal planes (m)
- $\theta$ = diffraction angle
- $n$ = integer (order)

## Examples
1. 50 g golf ball at 20 m/s: $\lambda = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-34}}{(0.05)(20)} = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}$ m (unmeasurably small)
2. Electron at 100 V: $\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2m_e eV}} = 0.123$ nm (comparable to interatomic distances)
