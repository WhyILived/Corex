# Test2: Miller Indices and Crystal Planes
> Tests: Learn2 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. What is the difference between [uvw] and ⟨uvw⟩ notation?

2. For a plane with intercepts at x = a/2, y = b, z = ∞c:
   - What are the Miller indices of this plane?
   - Sketch the relationship between this plane and the [210] direction in a cubic unit cell.

3. Why are {111} planes the most densely packed in FCC crystals?

4. In a cubic crystal, explain why the direction [hkℓ] is perpendicular to the plane (hkℓ).

## Calculation Problems

1. **Identify the plane**: A plane cuts the x-axis at a/2, the y-axis at b, and is parallel to the z-axis. What are its Miller indices?

2. **Planar concentration**: In an FCC crystal with a = 0.362 nm:
   - Which plane has higher atomic density: (100) or (110)?
   - Explain qualitatively why {111} planes are the most densely packed in FCC crystals.

3. **Family identification**: How many distinct directions are in the ⟨110⟩ family in a cubic crystal? List them.

## Answers

1. **[uvw]** denotes a specific direction; **⟨uvw⟩** denotes a family of crystallographically equivalent directions.

2. **Miller indices**: 
   - x₁ = 1/2, y₁ = 1, z₁ = ∞
   - Reciprocals: 2, 1, 0
   - Miller indices: **(210)**
   - This plane is perpendicular to the [210] direction in cubic crystals.

3. **{111} are most densely packed** because these planes have the highest planar concentration of atoms. The {111} planes in FCC have n₁₁₁ ≈ 17.0 atoms/nm² compared to n₁₀₀ ≈ 15.3 atoms/nm² and n₁₁₀ ≈ 10.8 atoms/nm².

4. In a cubic crystal, the Miller indices (hkℓ) are defined such that the direction [hkℓ] is perpendicular to the plane (hkℓ). This is a geometric consequence of how Miller indices are defined via reciprocal intercepts.

5. **Plane identification**: (210)
   - Intercepts: x = a/2, y = b, z = ∞
   - Reciprocals: 2, 1, 0
   - Miller indices: (210)

6. **Planar concentration**:
   - n₁₀₀ = 2/a² = 2/(0.362)² = 15.3 atoms/nm²
   - n₁₁₀ = 2/(a²√2) = 2/(0.362² × 1.414) = 10.8 atoms/nm²
   - n₁₀₀ > n₁₁₀

7. **⟨110⟩ family** in cubic crystals has 12 equivalent directions. All members are crystallographically equivalent by symmetry.
