# Learn2: Miller Indices and Crystal Planes
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: Crystal Directions

### Direction Notation
- Crystal directions are denoted by **square brackets [uvw]**
- Directions are expressed as the smallest integers with u, v, w being the vector components along x, y, z axes
- Example: A vector with components (1/2)a, b, (1/2)c along axes gives [121]

### Family of Directions
- **Triangular brackets ⟨uvw⟩** denote families of equivalent directions
- Example: ⟨100⟩ represents [100], [010], [001], [100], [010], [001] in cubic crystals
- Example: ⟨111⟩ represents all body diagonal directions

## Section 2: Crystal Planes

### Miller Indices Notation
- Crystal planes are denoted by **parentheses (hkℓ)**
- Miller indices are defined via the **reciprocals of the intercepts** the plane makes with the crystallographic axes
- If a plane intercepts at x₁, y₁, z₁ (in terms of lattice parameters a, b, c), the Miller indices are (1/x₁, 1/y₁, 1/z₁)

### How to Determine Miller Indices:
1. Find the intercepts of the plane on x, y, z axes (in terms of a, b, c)
2. Take reciprocals of these intercepts
3. Clear fractions (multiply to get smallest integers)
4. Put in parentheses without commas: (hkℓ)

### Example: (210) Plane
- Intercepts at x = a/2, y = b, z = ∞
- Reciprocals: 1/(1/2) = 2, 1/1 = 1, 1/∞ = 0
- Miller indices: **(210)**

### Negative Indices
- A negative intercept is indicated by a **bar** over the integer
- Example: The plane cutting the negative x-axis at a has Miller indices (100)

## Section 3: Important Plane Families in Cubic Crystals

### {100} Planes
- Face planes of the cube
- Example: (100), (010), (001), and their parallels

### {110} Planes
- Diagonal planes cutting two axes
- Example: (110), (101), (011)

### {111} Planes
- Diagonal planes cutting all three axes
- Example: (111), (11̄1), (1̄11), etc.

### Key Property
In a cubic crystal, the **[hkℓ] direction is always perpendicular to the (hkℓ) plane**

## Section 4: Planar Concentration
- **Planar concentration** refers to the number of atoms per unit area on a given (hkℓ) plane
- Among {100}, {110}, and {111} planes in FCC crystals:
  - **{111} planes are most densely packed**
  - **{110} planes are least densely packed**
- The exact planar concentration values can be derived from the crystal geometry
