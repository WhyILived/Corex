# Learn6: Energy Bands in Solids
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: From Atoms to N-Atom System

### Three H-Atom System
When three H atoms come together:
- Three 1s atomic orbitals → three molecular orbitals (ψa, ψb, ψc)
- Three different energies: Ea < Eb < Ec (more nodes = higher energy)
- Three electrons: first two pair in ψa (lowest), third enters ψb (next higher)

### N-Li Atom System (Analogy for Metals)
Lithium has electronic structure 1s²2s¹:
- The 1s subshell is full — stays close to nucleus, negligible splitting
- The 2s orbital is half-filled — splits into N finely separated energy levels
- These N levels form an **energy band** (continuum of ~10²³ levels)
- The energy levels are so finely separated that they form a nearly continuous band

## Section 2: Energy Band Formation

### Band Structure
- **EB** = bottom of the band
- **ET** = top of the band
- **Maximum splitting width** depends on closest interatomic distance a
- Each energy level Ei in the band corresponds to a different **wavefunction ψsolid(i)** — a different combination of atomic orbitals

### Why N Levels from N Orbitals?
- Each of N atomic orbitals can combine with different phases
- All N summed in phase → lowest energy wavefunction
- All N summed with alternating phases → highest energy wavefunction
- All other combinations → energies between EB and ET
- With N ≈ 10²³, the energy levels are **essentially continuous** — a continuum of states

## Section 3: Energy Bands in Metals

### Band Overlap
- The 2s band splits into N levels
- The 2p, 3s, 3p etc. also split into bands
- **In metals, energy bands often overlap**
- When bands overlap, electrons can move between bands, facilitating electrical conduction

### Li Metal Example
- N Li atoms → N 2s electrons
- 2s band has 2N states (spin up + spin down for each level)
- N electrons fill N/2 lowest levels → **band is half full**
- Empty states above allow electrons to gain energy and conduct

## Section 4: Fermi Level

At absolute zero (0 K):
- All electrons fill energy levels from EB up to **EFO** (Fermi level)
- EFO is the energy of the highest occupied state at 0 K
- Electrons at EFO have the **Fermi energy**

The work function Φ:
- Energy required to excite an electron from Fermi level to vacuum level
- Φ = EF (if vacuum level is reference 0)

## Key Equations

**Number of states in band from N atomic orbitals:**
$$2N \text{ states (spin up + spin down)}$$

**Band half-filling:**
$$\text{If N electrons per N atoms } \rightarrow \text{band is } \frac{1}{2} \text{ full}$$

**Fermi energy for Li metal:**
$$E_{FO} = 4.7 \text{ eV (measured from bottom of band)}$$

**Work function:**
$$\Phi = \text{energy to remove electron from } E_F \text{ to vacuum level}$$

**Energy band width:**
$$\Delta E = E_T - E_B \sim 10 \text{ eV for typical metals}$$

Where:
- N ≈ 10²³ atoms (Avogadro's number scale)
- The energy levels are "finely separated" — effectively continuous for practical purposes
