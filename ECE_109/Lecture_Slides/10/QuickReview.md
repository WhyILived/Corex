## Crystal Structure Summary

| Crystal System | Lattice Parameters | Examples |
|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Cubic | a = b = c, α = β = γ = 90° | Al, Cu, Fe, Pb |
| Tetragonal | a = b ≠ c, α = β = γ = 90° | In, Sn, TiO₂ |
| Orthorhombic | a ≠ b ≠ c, α = β = γ = 90° | S, U, Ga |
| Rhombohedral | a = b = c, α = β = γ ≠ 90° | As, Bi, Sb |
| Hexagonal | a = b ≠ c, α = β = 90°, γ = 120° | Mg, Zn, graphite |
| Monoclinic | a ≠ b ≠ c, α = β = 90°, γ ≠ 90° | α-Se, P |
| Triclinic | a ≠ b ≠ c, α ≠ β ≠ γ ≠ 90° | K₂Cr₂O₇ |

## 14 Bravais Lattices
- 3 cubic (simple, BCC, FCC)
- 2 tetragonal (simple, body-centered)
- 4 orthorhombic (simple, body-centered, base-centered, face-centered)
- 1 rhombohedral
- 1 hexagonal
- 2 monoclinic (simple, base-centered)
- 1 triclinic

## Crystal Defects
- **Vacancies**: Missing atoms, thermodynamic equilibrium
- **Substitutional impurities**: Foreign atoms replacing host atoms
- **Interstitial impurities**: Foreign atoms in void spaces
- **Grain boundaries**: Where crystals with different orientations meet
- **Surface defects**: Dangling bonds, surface reconstruction

## Miller Indices
- **[uvw]** = direction notation
- **⟨uvw⟩** = family of equivalent directions
- **(hkℓ)** = plane notation (Miller indices)
- **{hkl}** = family of equivalent planes
- [hkℓ] ⊥ (hkℓ) in cubic crystals

## Czochralski Growth
- Seed crystal lowered to molten Si
- Slow pulling + rotation → single crystal ingot
- Produces wafers for IC fabrication

## Amorphous Solids
- **No long-range order** (unlike crystals)
- **Short-range order**: local bonding satisfied
- **Continuous random network (CRN)** model
- Glass = amorphous solid from rapid cooling

## Molecular Orbital Theory
- Atomic orbitals combine via LCAO
- **ψσ** = bonding orbital (lower energy, electron density between nuclei)
- **ψσ* = antibonding orbital** (higher energy, node between nuclei)
- Bonding requires half-occupied orbitals

## Energy Band Theory
- N atoms → N energy levels split from each atomic level
- ~10²³ levels create essentially continuous bands
- **Fermi level EFO**: highest occupied state at 0 K
- **Work function Φ**: energy to remove electron from EF to vacuum
- Metals: bands overlap, partially filled
- Insulators: band gap too large to excite electrons

## Key Numbers to Remember
- Fermi energy of Li: 4.7 eV
- Work function of Li: 2.9 eV
- FCC packing factor: 0.74 (74%)
- Planck constant: h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
- Boltzmann constant: k = 8.617 × 10⁻⁵ eV/K
