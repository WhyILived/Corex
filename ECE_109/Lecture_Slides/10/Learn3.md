# Learn3: Crystalline Defects
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: Point Defects

### Vacancies
- A **vacancy** is a missing atom from its lattice site
- Vacancies are **thermodynamic defects** — they exist at all temperatures above absolute zero due to thermal equilibrium
- Vacancy concentration increases exponentially with temperature

### Substitutional Impurities
- A **substitutional impurity** replaces a host atom on a lattice site
- Can be larger or smaller than the host atom, causing lattice strain
- Examples: As in Si, Cu in Al

### Interstitial Impurities
- An **interstitial impurity** occupies the void space between host atoms
- Small atoms (like C in Fe) typically occupy interstitial sites
- Also causes lattice distortion and strain

### Vacancy Concentration Equation
$$n_v = N \exp\left(-\frac{E_v}{kT}\right)$$

Where:
- $n_v$ = vacancy concentration (number per unit volume)
- $N$ = total number of atomic sites per unit volume
- $E_v$ = energy required to create a vacancy (eV)
- $k$ = Boltzmann constant ($8.617 \times 10^{-5}$ eV/K)
- $T$ = absolute temperature (K)

## Section 2: Planar Defects

### Grain Boundaries
- **Grain boundaries** occur where crystals with different orientations meet during solidification
- Atoms at grain boundaries have **broken bonds, voids, and strained bonds**
- Grain boundaries are high-energy regions that facilitate atomic diffusion
- Polycrystalline materials have smaller grains with more grain boundary area

### Crystal Surfaces
- When the crystal lattice is terminated by a surface, atoms cannot satisfy their bonding requirements
- Surface atoms have **dangling bonds** — half-filled bonds looking for partners
- Surface atoms may **reconstruct** by bonding with neighboring surface atoms
- The crystal surface can have various features including: steps, vacancies (missing surface atoms), and adatoms (atoms on the surface)



## Key Points
- There is no such thing as a perfect crystal — defects exist naturally
- Defects control many important mechanical and electrical properties
- Vacancies follow thermal equilibrium statistics (Boltzmann distribution)
- Grain boundaries and surfaces are high-energy regions due to unsatisfied bonds
