# Test5: Molecular Orbitals and Bonding in H₂
> Tests: Learn5 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. Why does the 1s energy level split into two levels when two H atoms approach each other?

2. Distinguish between ψσ and ψσ* in terms of:
   - Symmetry
   - Electron probability distribution
   - Energy level relative to E₁s

3. What happens when we bring three hydrogen atoms together instead of two? How does the energy level structure change?

4. What is the physical origin of the energy splitting when two atoms form a molecule?

## Calculation Problems

1. **Energy diagram**: Sketch the energy levels for:
   - Two isolated H atoms at R = ∞
   - The H₂ molecule at equilibrium separation R = a
   - Label E₁s, Eσ, Eσ*, bonding energy ΔE

2. **Orbital occupancy**: For the H₂ molecule:
   - How many molecular orbitals are formed from two 1s atomic orbitals?
   - What is the maximum number of electrons each can hold?
   - In the ground state, which orbitals are occupied and with how many electrons?

## Answers

1. **Energy level splitting**: Atomic orbitals overlap when atoms approach. This overlap leads to two different ways the wavefunctions can combine — in phase (bonding) or out of phase (antibonding). These two combinations have different energies because the electron probability distributions differ.

2. **ψσ vs ψσ***:
   | Property | ψσ (Bonding) | ψσ* (Antibonding) |
   |----------|--------------|-------------------|
   | Symmetry | Symmetric (same sign on both nuclei) | Antisymmetric (opposite signs) |
   | Electron density | High between nuclei | Zero between nuclei (node) |
   | Energy | Lower than E₁s | Higher than E₁s |
   | Effect on bonding | Stabilizes molecule | Destabilizes/prevents bonding |

3. **3 H atoms**: When three H atoms come together:
   - Three 1s atomic orbitals combine to form three molecular orbitals with three different energies
   - The energies ordered from lowest to highest: Ea < Eb < Ec (more nodes = higher energy)
   - Three electrons: first two pair in ψa (lowest), third enters ψb (next higher)

4. **Physical origin of splitting**: The interaction (overlap) between atomic orbitals causes the energy levels to split. When atoms are far apart, each has discrete energy levels. As they approach, the wavefunctions overlap and combine in different ways, leading to different energies.

5. **Orbital occupancy**:
   - 2 molecular orbitals formed from 2 1s atomic orbitals
   - Each holds max 2 electrons
   - Ground state: ψσ holds 2 electrons (paired spins), ψσ* is empty
