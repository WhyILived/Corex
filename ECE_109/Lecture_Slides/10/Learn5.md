# Learn5: Molecular Orbitals and Bonding in H₂
> Estimated time: ~30 minutes

## Section 1: The Hydrogen Molecule Problem

When two H atoms approach each other to form H₂:
- Each isolated H atom has electron energy = -13.6 eV (in 1s orbital)
- The two H atoms together have energy 2 × (-13.6 eV) = -27.2 eV when infinitely separated
- As atoms approach, electrons interact with both nuclei
- The total energy of the system becomes **lower** than -27.2 eV — bonding is favorable

## Section 2: Molecular Orbital Theory

### Linear Combination of Atomic Orbitals (LCAO)
The molecular orbital ψ is formed from a linear combination of atomic wavefunctions:

$$\psi_\sigma = \psi_{1s}(r_A) + \psi_{1s}(r_B)$$

$$\psi_\sigma^* = \psi_{1s}(r_A) - \psi_{1s}(r_B)$$

### Bonding Orbital (ψσ)
- **Symmetric** — wavefunction has same sign on both nuclei
- Has **considerable electron density between the two nuclei**
- **Lower energy** than atomic 1s level
- Can hold 2 electrons with paired spins (Pauli Exclusion Principle)

### Antibonding Orbital (ψσ*)
- **Antisymmetric** — wavefunction has opposite signs on the two nuclei
- Has a **node (zero probability) between the two nuclei**
- **Higher energy** than atomic 1s level
- When occupied, weakens or prevents bonding

## Section 3: Energy Diagram

At interatomic separation R = ∞:
- Two separate H atoms, each with 1s energy level

As R decreases (atoms approach):
- The 1s energy level **splits into two levels**: Eσ (bonding) and Eσ* (antibonding)
- Eσ drops below E1s, Eσ* rises above E1s
- The splitting occurs due to **overlap/interaction between atomic orbitals**

At equilibrium separation R = a:
- Two electrons (one from each H atom) occupy ψσ orbital
- This is the **bonding energy ΔE** — energy difference between two isolated H atoms and the H₂ molecule minimum
- This is the hydrogen molecule H₂

## Section 4: Generalization to N Atoms

The concepts from H₂ can be extended to more atoms:
- With 3 H atoms, we get 3 molecular orbitals with different energies
- As the number of atoms increases to N (~10²³), the energy levels become finely separated, forming an **energy band**
- The splitting of energy levels when atoms form molecules/solids is a general quantum mechanical phenomenon

## Key Equations

**Bonding molecular orbital:**
$$\psi_\sigma = \psi_{1s}(r_A) + \psi_{1s}(r_B)$$

**Antibonding molecular orbital:**
$$\psi_\sigma^* = \psi_{1s}(r_A) - \psi_{1s}(r_B)$$

**Bonding energy:**
$$\Delta E = E_{H_2} - 2E_{H}$$
