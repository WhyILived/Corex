# Test4: Crystalline State and Bravais Lattices
> Tests: Learn4 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. Distinguish between a lattice, a basis, and a crystal. How do these three concepts relate to each other?

2. What is the difference between a primitive unit cell and a Wigner-Seitz cell?

3. How many Bravais lattices exist in 3D? Into how many crystal systems are they grouped?

4. Why does the diamond structure have a two-atom basis?

## Calculation Problems

1. **Bravais Lattice Counting**: List all 7 crystal systems and state how many Bravais lattices belong to each system.

2. **Coordination Number Comparison**: 
   - What is the coordination number for FCC? For BCC?
   - Which structure (FCC or BCC) has higher atomic packing factor and why?

3. **Diamond Structure**: In the diamond cubic structure:
   - What is the underlying Bravais lattice?
   - If the lattice constant is a, what is the distance between nearest neighbor atoms?

## Answers

1. **Bravais Lattice Counting**:

   | Crystal System | Number of Bravais Lattices |
   |----------------|---------------------------|
   | Cubic | 3 (SC, BCC, FCC) |
   | Tetragonal | 2 (simple, body-centered) |
   | Orthorhombic | 4 (simple, body-centered, base-centered, face-centered) |
   | Hexagonal | 1 |
   | Rhombohedral | 1 |
   | Monoclinic | 2 (simple, base-centered) |
   | Triclinic | 1 |
   | **Total** | **14** |

2. **Coordination Numbers**:
   - FCC: Coordination number = 12 (each atom touches 12 nearest neighbors)
   - BCC: Coordination number = 8 (each atom touches 8 nearest neighbors)
   
   FCC has higher atomic packing factor because:
   - FCC efficiency ≈ 74%
   - BCC efficiency ≈ 68%
   
   This is because in FCC, atoms are arranged in more closely packed layers.

3. **Diamond Structure**:
   - The underlying Bravais lattice is **Face-Centered Cubic (FCC)**
   - The two-atom basis consists of atoms at (0,0,0) and (1/4, 1/4, 1/4)a
   - Nearest neighbor distance = $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}a$ (the distance from an atom at corner to the atom at body center of the tetrahedron)
