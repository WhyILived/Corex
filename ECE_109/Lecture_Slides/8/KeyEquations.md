# KeyEquations8: Important Equations from Lecture 8

## Quantum Mechanics

### Schrödinger Equation (3D)
$$\frac{\partial^2\psi}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2\psi}{\partial y^2} + \frac{\partial^2\psi}{\partial z^2} + \frac{2m_e}{\hbar^2}(E - V)\psi = 0$$

### Coulomb Potential (Hydrogen)
$$V(r) = -\frac{Ze^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r}$$

### Hydrogen Wavefunction
$$\psi_{n,\ell,m_\ell}(r, \theta, \phi) = R_{n,\ell}(r) \cdot Y_{\ell,m_\ell}(\theta, \phi)$$

### Quantum Numbers
- $n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$ (principal)
- $\ell = 0, 1, 2, \ldots, (n-1)$ (orbital angular momentum)
- $m_\ell = -\ell, \ldots, +\ell$ (magnetic)

### Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle
$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p_x \gtrsim \hbar$$

Where $\hbar = \frac{h}{2\pi}$

## Atomic Structure

### Atomic Mass Unit
$$u = 1.66054 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$$

### Avogadro's Number
$$N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1}$$

## Bonding and Energy

### Ionic Bond Potential Energy
$$E(r) = -\frac{M e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r} + \frac{B}{r^m}$$

Where:
- $M$ = Madelung constant (NaCl: M = 1.748)
- $B$ = repulsive constant
- $m$ = exponent (typically 8-10)

### Equilibrium Condition
$$\frac{dE}{dr} = 0 \text{ at } r = r_0$$

### Cohesive Energy (NaCl example)
$$E_{bond} = |E_{min}| + \text{ionization energy} + |\text{electron affinity}|$$
$$E_{bond} = 7.84 \text{ eV} + 5.14 \text{ eV} - 3.61 \text{ eV} = 6.31 \text{ eV}$$

### Lattice Energy Conversion
$$E_{cohesive} = (6.31 \text{ eV}) \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV}) \times (6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1}) = 608 \text{ kJ/mol}$$

## Crystallography

### Lattice Vector
$$\vec{R} = h\hat{a} + k\hat{b} + l\hat{c}$$

### Lattice Parameters
- $a, b, c$: edge lengths
- $\alpha$: angle between b and c
- $\beta$: angle between a and c
- $\gamma$: angle between a and b
