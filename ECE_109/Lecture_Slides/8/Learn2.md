## Section 1: Atomic Structure

### Nucleus
The nucleus contains:
- **Protons**: positively charged particles, number = Z (atomic number)
- **Neutrons**: electrically neutral particles
- **Atomic mass number (A)**: total number of protons + neutrons

### Electron Shells
Electrons occupy shells (also called energy levels):
- K shell: innermost (n = 1)
- L shell: second (n = 2)
- M shell: third (n = 3)

Each shell can contain subshells (s, p, d, f) determined by the orbital angular momentum quantum number ℓ.

### Electron Configuration Notation
The electron configuration indicates how electrons are distributed among shells and subshells.

Example - Carbon (Z = 6): $1s^2 2s^2 2p^2$ or [He]$2s^2 2p^2$

## Section 2: Ionization Energy

**Definition**: The minimum energy required to remove a single electron from a neutral atom, creating a positive ion (cation) and a free electron.

$$Na \rightarrow Na^+ + e^-$$

The ionization energy of sodium is 5.1 eV - meaning 5.1 eV of energy is needed to remove the outermost electron.

**Key point**: Ionization energy is always positive (endothermic process).

## Section 3: Electron Affinity

**Definition**: The energy change when an electron is added to a neutral atom to create a negative ion (anion).

$$Cl + e^- \rightarrow Cl^-$$

For chlorine: -3.6 eV (negative value means energy is released - exothermic process)

**Key point**: Electron affinity can be positive (energy absorbed) or negative (energy released).

## Section 4: The Mole and Avogadro's Number

### Atomic Mass Unit
$$u = 1.66054 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$$

Definition: 1/12 of the mass of a neutral carbon atom with A = 12 (the isotope with 6 protons and 6 neutrons).

### Avogadro's Number
$$N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1}$$

Definition: The number of atoms in exactly 12 grams of carbon-12 isotope.

### The Mole
A **mole** of a substance contains exactly $N_A$ atoms or molecules.

## Section 5: Weight Fraction and Atomic Fraction Conversion

For a two-element alloy or compound (elements A and B):

**Weight fractions** (commonly used in materials engineering):
$$w_A + w_B = 1$$

**Atomic (molar) fractions** (commonly used in chemistry):
$$n_A + n_B = 1$$

**Conversion formulas**:
$$n_A = \frac{w_A / M_A}{w_A / M_A + w_B / M_B}$$

$$n_B = 1 - n_A$$

Where $M_A$ and $M_B$ are the atomic masses of elements A and B.

**Reverse conversion**:
$$w_A = \frac{n_A \cdot M_A}{n_A \cdot M_A + n_B \cdot M_B}$$
