# QuickReview8: Concise Summary of Lecture 8

## Quantum States of Hydrogen Atom
- Three quantum numbers (n, ℓ, mℓ) arise from solving the Schrödinger equation
- Wavefunction separates into radial (R) and angular (Y) parts
- Electron probability distribution given by $|R|^2 |Y|^2$

## Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle
- $\Delta x \cdot \Delta p_x \gtrsim \hbar$
- Explains why electron cannot merge with proton - cannot have precise position and momentum simultaneously

## Atomic Structure
- **Z** = atomic number (protons)
- **A** = atomic mass number (protons + neutrons)
- Ionization energy: energy to remove electron (always positive/endothermic)
- Electron affinity: energy change when adding electron (can be positive or negative)

## The Mole
- $N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23}$ mol$^{-1}$
- $u = 1.66054 \times 10^{-27}$ kg
- Weight fraction ↔ Atomic fraction conversion using atomic masses

## Bonding Types in Solids
| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Ionic | Electron transfer, Coulomb attraction | NaCl |
| Covalent | Electron sharing | Diamond, Si |
| Metallic | Delocalized electron gas | Cu, Mg |
| van der Waals | Dipole-dipole attraction | Ice, Ar crystal |

## Crystalline State
- **Lattice**: infinite periodic array of points
- **Basis**: group of atoms at each lattice point
- **Crystal** = Lattice + Basis

## Bravais Lattices (14 total, 7 systems)
- Cubic (3): SC, BCC, FCC
- Tetragonal (2)
- Orthorhombic (4)
- Hexagonal (1)
- Rhombohedral (1)
- Monoclinic (2)
- Triclinic (1)

## Diamond Structure
- FCC lattice with 2-atom basis
- Tetrahedral coordination (4 nearest neighbors)
- Examples: Si, Ge, C (diamond)
