# Test3: Blackbody Radiation and Planck's Law
> Tests: Learn3 material
> Time: ~15 minutes

## Conceptual Questions

1. Explain why a small hole in a hollow heated object acts as a good approximation of a blackbody.

2. The "ultraviolet catastrophe" was a problem in classical physics. What did it predict, and why was it wrong?

3. Using Wien's displacement law, explain why objects appear to change color as they are heated (e.g., from red-hot to white-hot).

4. Two blackbodies are at temperatures 2000 K and 4000 K. By what factor does the total radiated power increase when the temperature doubles?

## Calculation Problems

1. **Wien's Displacement Law**
   
   The Sun's surface temperature is approximately 5800 K. Calculate the wavelength of maximum spectral irradiance.
   
   Expected answer: $\lambda_{max} \approx 500$ nm (visible green light)

2. **Stefan-Boltzmann Law**
   
   Calculate the total power radiated per unit area by a blackbody at room temperature $T = 300$ K.
   
   Given: $\sigma = 5.67 \times 10^{-8}$ W/m²/K⁴
   
   Expected answer: $P_S \approx 460$ W/m²

3. **Peak Wavelength Shift**
   
   A heated iron bar appears red-hot with $\lambda_{max} \approx 700$ nm. Estimate its temperature.
   
   Expected answer: $T \approx 4100$ K

4. **Power Ratio**
   
   A blackbody at $T_1 = 1000$ K radiates a total power $P_1$ per unit area. What temperature $T_2$ would be needed to make the radiated power 16 times greater?
   
   Expected answer: $T_2 = 2000$ K (since power scales as $T^4$)

## Answers

1. **Sun's peak wavelength**:
   
   $\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.89 \times 10^{-3}}{5800} = 4.98 \times 10^{-7}$ m $\approx 500$ nm

2. **Room temperature radiation**:
   
   $P_S = \sigma T^4 = (5.67 \times 10^{-8})(300)^4 = 459$ W/m² $\approx 460$ W/m²

3. **Red-hot iron temperature**:
   
   $T = \frac{2.89 \times 10^{-3}}{700 \times 10^{-9}} = 4130$ K $\approx 4100$ K

4. **Power ratio temperature**:
   
   $\frac{P_2}{P_1} = \left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right)^4 = 16$
   
   $\frac{T_2}{T_1} = 16^{1/4} = 2$
   
   $T_2 = 2 \times 1000 = 2000$ K
