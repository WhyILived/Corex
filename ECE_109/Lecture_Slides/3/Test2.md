# Test2: Photoelectric Effect
> Tests: Learn2 material

## Conceptual Questions

1. In the photoelectric effect, explain why increasing the light intensity does not increase the kinetic energy of emitted electrons, even though it increases the number of electrons emitted.

2. The work function of sodium is approximately 2.3 eV. Will visible light (wavelength 400-700 nm) cause photoelectric emission from sodium? Show your reasoning.

3. Two different metals are illuminated with the same frequency of light. Metal A emits electrons with higher maximum kinetic energy than Metal B. What can you conclude about the work functions of these metals?

4. Explain what is meant by "stopping potential" in the context of the photoelectric effect.

## Calculation Problems

1. **Work Function and Kinetic Energy**
   
   Light with wavelength $\lambda = 400$ nm strikes a metal with work function $\Phi = 2.0$ eV.
   
   Calculate:
   (a) The photon energy in eV
   (b) The maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons in eV
   
   Given: $h = 6.6 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s, $1$ eV $= 1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ J, $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s
   
   Expected answer: (a) $E \approx 3.1$ eV, (b) $KE_{max} \approx 1.1$ eV

2. **Threshold Frequency**
   
   The work function of cesium is $\Phi = 1.9$ eV. Calculate the threshold frequency for photoelectric emission from cesium.
   
   Expected answer: $f \approx 4.6 \times 10^{14}$ Hz

3. **Stopping Potential**
   
   When light of frequency $f = 8.0 \times 10^{14}$ Hz illuminates a metal, the stopping potential is $V_0 = 1.2$ V. Calculate the work function of this metal in eV.
   
   Expected answer: $\Phi \approx 2.1$ eV

4. **Electron Velocity**
   
   In a photoelectric experiment, the stopping potential is $V_0 = 3.5$ V for light of wavelength $\lambda = 300$ nm. Calculate the maximum velocity of the emitted electrons.
   
   Given: $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg, $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C
   
   Expected answer: $v \approx 1.1 \times 10^6$ m/s

## Answers

1. (a) $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{(6.6 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{400 \times 10^{-9}} = 4.95 \times 10^{-19}$ J
   
   In eV: $E = \frac{4.95 \times 10^{-19}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 3.1$ eV
   
   (b) $KE_{max} = E - \Phi = 3.1$ eV $- 2.0$ eV $= 1.1$ eV

2. $f_0 = \frac{\Phi}{h} = \frac{(1.9 \text{ eV})(1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV})}{6.6 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J·s}} = 4.6 \times 10^{14}$ Hz

3. $KE_{max} = eV_0 = (1)(1.2) = 1.2$ eV
   
   From $KE_{max} = hf - \Phi$:
   
   $\Phi = hf - KE_{max} = \frac{(8.0 \times 10^{14})(6.6 \times 10^{-34})}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} - 1.2$ eV $= 3.3 - 1.2 = 2.1$ eV

4. $KE_{max} = eV_0 = (1.6 \times 10^{-19})(3.5) = 5.6 \times 10^{-19}$ J
   
   $v_{max} = \sqrt{\frac{2KE_{max}}{m_e}} = \sqrt{\frac{2(5.6 \times 10^{-19})}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}} \approx 1.1 \times 10^6$ m/s
