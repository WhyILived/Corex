## Conceptual Questions

1. In Young's double-slit experiment, what happens to the interference pattern if the phase difference between the two slits is zero? Describe whether you would see constructive or destructive interference.

2. Explain why $|cos(x) + cos(y)|^2 \neq |cos(x)|^2 + |cos(y)|^2$. What phenomenon does this mathematical fact represent?

3. Light intensity is measured as power per unit area (W/m²). Using the photon picture, explain two different ways to increase the intensity of a light beam.

4. Two light beams have the same wavelength but different intensities. How does the momentum of photons compare between the two beams? Explain your reasoning.

## Calculation Problems

1. **Photon Energy Calculation**
   
   Calculate the energy of a photon with wavelength $\lambda = 620$ nm.
   
   Given: $h = 6.6 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s, $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s
   
   Expected answer: $E \approx 3.2 \times 10^{-19}$ J (or ~2 eV)

2. **Interference Path Difference**
   
   In a Young's double-slit setup, light of wavelength $\lambda = 550$ nm produces a bright fringe at a point where the path difference is $S1P - S2P = 2.75$ μm.
   
   Determine the order $n$ of this bright fringe.
   
   Expected answer: $n = 5$

3. **Photon Momentum**
   
   An X-ray photon has wavelength $\lambda = 0.1$ nm. Calculate its momentum.
   
   Given: $h = 6.6 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s
   
   Expected answer: $p \approx 6.6 \times 10^{-24}$ kg·m/s

4. **Intensity and Photon Flux**
   
   A laser beam with wavelength $\lambda = 600$ nm produces an intensity of $I = 100$ W/m² on a surface. Calculate the photon flux density (number of photons per second per square meter).
   
   Given: $h = 6.6 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s, $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s
   
   Expected answer: $\Gamma_{ph} \approx 3.0 \times 10^{20}$ photons/m²/s

## Answers

1. **Photon Energy**: $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{(6.6 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{620 \times 10^{-9}} = 3.19 \times 10^{-19}$ J $\approx 2.0$ eV

2. **Interference Order**: Since $n = \frac{S1P - S2P}{\lambda} = \frac{2.75 \times 10^{-6}}{550 \times 10^{-9}} = 5$, this is the 5th order bright fringe.

3. **X-ray Momentum**: $p = \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{6.6 \times 10^{-34}}{0.1 \times 10^{-9}} = 6.6 \times 10^{-24}$ kg·m/s

4. **Photon Flux**: $\Gamma_{ph} = \frac{I}{hf} = \frac{I\lambda}{hc} = \frac{(100)(600 \times 10^{-9})}{(6.6 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)} = 3.03 \times 10^{20}$ photons/m²/s
