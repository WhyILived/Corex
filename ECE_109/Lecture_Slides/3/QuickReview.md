## Wave-Particle Duality of Light

**Young's Double-Slit Experiment** demonstrates light's wave nature through interference patterns:
- Constructive interference: $S1P - S2P = n\lambda$
- Destructive interference: $S1P - S2P = (n + \frac{1}{2})\lambda$

**Photons** are particles of light with quantized energy and momentum:
- $E = hf = h/\lambda$
- $p = h/\lambda$ (zero mass but carries momentum)

**Applications**: Radiation pressure, solar sails, optical tweezers

## Photoelectric Effect

Light behaves as particles (photons) in the photoelectric effect:
- $KE_{max} = hf - \Phi_{metal}$
- Electron energy depends on frequency (color), not intensity
- Different metals have different work functions

## Blackbody Radiation

**Planck's Law** resolved the ultraviolet catastrophe:
- $I_\lambda = \frac{2\pi hc^2}{\lambda^5[exp(hc/\lambda kT) - 1]}$
- Energy is quantized into photon-like packets

**Key results**:
- Total power: $P_S = \sigma T^4$ (Stefan-Boltzmann)
- Peak wavelength: $\lambda_{max} \approx \frac{2.89 \times 10^{-3}}{T}$ m·K

## De Broglie Waves

Particles exhibit wave behavior (matter waves):
- $\lambda = h/p$ (de Broglie relation)
- **Electron diffraction** confirms particles can behave as waves
- Young's double-slit experiment works with electrons too

## Quantum Mechanics Basics

**Wavefunction** $\Psi$ describes quantum particles:
- Complex-valued, normed ($\int|\Psi|^2 dV = 1$)
- $|\Psi|^2$ gives probability density (physical observable)

**Schrödinger Equation** is the equation of motion for quantum systems:

1. **Time-dependent form**:
   $$j\hbar\frac{d}{dt}|\psi\rangle = \hat{H}|\psi\rangle$$

2. **Time-independent form** (eigenvalue equation):
   $$\hat{H}\psi = E\psi$$

Where $\hat{H} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2 + V$ is the Hamiltonian operator.

**Free particle** ($V=0$): $\psi(x) = Ae^{jkx}$ — a traveling wave solution

**Key concepts**: Operators, eigenfunctions, stationary states, time evolution
