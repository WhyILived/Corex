# KeyEquations: Lecture 9

## Bonding and Forces (Review from Lecture 4)
- Force-distance curves: attractive (FA), repulsive (FR), net force (FN = FA + FR)
- Potential energy curves: EA (attractive PE), ER (repulsive energy), E = EA + ER
- Equilibrium separation: r₀ where dE/dr = 0

## Ionic Bonding Energy
$$E(r) = -\frac{e^2 M}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r} + \frac{B}{r^m}$$

Where:
- M = Madelung constant (1.748 for NaCl)
- m = 8 for NaCl
- B = constant (6.972 × 10⁻⁹⁶ J·m⁸)
- r₀ determined by dE/dr = 0

## Lattice Parameters
- a, b, c: edge lengths
- α, β, γ: angles between edges
- α between b and c, β between a and c, γ between a and b

## FCC Relations
$$a = \frac{4R}{\sqrt{2}} = R \cdot 2\sqrt{2}$$

$$APF_{FCC} = 0.74$$

$$n_{at} = \frac{4}{a^3}$$

## BCC Relations
$$a = \frac{4R}{\sqrt{3}}$$

$$APF_{BCC} = 0.68$$

## Diamond Cubic
- 8 atoms per unit cell
- APF = 0.34
- Two-atom basis at (0,0,0) and (a/4, a/4, a/4)

## Atomic Packing Factor (General)
$$APF = \frac{\text{Volume of atoms in unit cell}}{\text{Volume of unit cell}}$$

## Density
$$\rho = \frac{n_{at} \cdot M_{at}}{N_A}$$

Where Nₐ = 6.022 × 10²³ mol⁻¹

## Vacancy Concentration
$$n_v = N \exp\left(\frac{-E_v}{kT}\right)$$

Where:
- Eᵥ = vacancy formation energy
- k = Boltzmann constant (8.617 × 10⁻⁵ eV/K)

## Crystal Directions and Planes
- Direction notation: [uvw] in square brackets
- Family of directions: ⟨uvw⟩ in triangular brackets
- Plane notation: (hkl) in parentheses
- Family of planes: {hkl} in curly brackets

## Miller Indices (for planes)
1. Find intercepts x₁, y₁, z₁ in terms of a, b, c
2. Take reciprocals: 1/x₁, 1/y₁, 1/z₁
3. Clear fractions to smallest integers (h, k, l)
4. Write as (hkl)
