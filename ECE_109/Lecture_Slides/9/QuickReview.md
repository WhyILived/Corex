# QuickReview9: Crystal Structure and Defects

## Crystal Structure Fundamentals

**Lattice + Basis = Crystal**

- **Lattice**: Infinite periodic array of geometric points (no atoms)
- **Basis**: Group of atoms at each lattice point
- **Unit cell**: Smallest repeating volume that can reproduce the crystal

**7 Crystal Systems** (based on lattice parameters):
- Cubic (a=b=c, α=β=γ=90°)
- Tetragonal, Orthorhombic, Rhombohedral, Hexagonal, Monoclinic, Triclinic

**14 Bravais Lattices** fill 3D space: SC, BCC, FCC (cubic); simple & body-centered tetragonal; simple, body-centered, base-centered, face-centered orthorhombic; rhombohedral, hexagonal, simple & base-centered monoclinic, triclinic.

## Common Structures

| Structure | Atoms/Unit Cell | Coordination | APF | Examples |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|-----|----------|
| SC | 1 | 6 | 0.52 | Po |
| BCC | 2 | 8 | 0.68 | α-Fe, Cr, Mo, W |
| FCC | 4 | 12 | 0.74 | Cu, Al, Au |
| Diamond | 8 | 4 | 0.34 | C, Si, Ge |
| Zinc Blende | 8 | 4 | 0.34 | GaAs, ZnS |

**Key relations**:
- FCC: a = 4R/√2, APF = 0.74
- BCC: a = 4R/√3, APF = 0.68
- Density: ρ = nₐₜMₐₜ/Nₐ

## Polymorphism (Allotropy)
Same substance can have multiple crystal structures:
- **Carbon**: Diamond (cubic, insulator), Graphite (hexagonal, conductor), C₆₀ (FCC, semiconductor)
- **Iron**: α-Fe (BCC < 912°C), γ-Fe (FCC 912-1400°C), δ-Fe (BCC > 1400°C)

## Crystal Defects

**Point Defects**: Vacancies, substitutional impurities, interstitial impurities
$$n_v = N \exp\left(\frac{-E_v}{kT}\right)$$

**Line Defects**: Edge dislocations, screw dislocations, misfit/threading dislocations

**Planar Defects**: Grain boundaries, surfaces (terraces, steps, ledges, kinks, adatoms, vacancies)

## Crystal Directions and Planes

- **Direction**: [uvw] (square brackets)
- **Family of directions**: ⟨uvw⟩
- **Plane**: (hkl)
- **Family of planes**: {hkl}
- **Cubic property**: [hkl] ⟂ (hkl)

**Miller indices for planes**: Take intercepts → reciprocals → clear fractions → (hkl)
