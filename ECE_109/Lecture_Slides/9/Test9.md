## Calculation Problems

1. **FCC Crystal Structure**: For a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal with atomic radius R = 0.128 nm:
   - Calculate the lattice parameter a
   - Calculate the atomic packing factor (APF)
   - Calculate the atomic concentration (number of atoms per cm³)
   - Calculate the density if the atomic mass is 63.55 g/mol

2. **BCC Crystal Structure**: For a body-centered cubic (BCC) crystal with lattice parameter a = 0.286 nm:
   - Calculate the number of atoms per unit cell
   - Calculate the atomic packing factor
   - Calculate the atomic radius R

3. **Vacancy Concentration**: Given N = 8.43 × 10²² atoms/cm³ and Eᵥ = 0.9 eV, calculate the vacancy concentration at T = 1000 K. (k = 8.617 × 10⁻⁵ eV/K)

4. **Miller Indices**:
   - Identify the direction [121] in a cubic unit cell
   - Identify the plane with intercepts at a/2, b, and ∞c
   - What family of directions does [111] belong to?

5. **Planar Concentration**: For an FCC crystal with a = 0.362 nm, calculate the planar concentration (atoms per nm²) on the (110) plane.

## Answers

1. **FCC Calculation**:
   - a = 4R/√2 = 0.362 nm
   - APF = 0.74
   - nₐₜ = 4/a³ = 8.43 × 10²² cm⁻³
   - ρ = (8.43 × 10²² × 63.55)/(6.022 × 10²³) = 8.9 g/cm³

2. **BCC Calculation**:
   - 2 atoms per unit cell
   - APF = 0.68
   - R = a√3/4 = 0.124 nm

3. **Vacancy Concentration**:
   - nᵥ = 8.43 × 10²² × exp(-0.9/(8.617 × 10⁻⁵ × 1000)) = 8.43 × 10²² × exp(-10.44) ≈ 1.9 × 10¹⁹ cm⁻³

4. **Miller Indices**:
   - [121] direction with coordinates at a/2, b, c/2
   - Plane (210)
   - [111] belongs to the ⟨111⟩ family

5. **Planar Concentration**:
   - n(110) = 2/(a²√2) = 10.8 atoms/nm²
